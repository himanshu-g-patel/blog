This is a curated list of books and articles that have shaped my thinking as an engineering leader. These resources are essential for staff engineers, engineering managers, and directors who want to grow their technical and leadership skills.

## Books

### Engineering Leadership & Management

**[The Manager's Path](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/)** by Camille Fournier
The definitive guide for engineering managers at all levels. Covers everything from being a tech lead to VP of Engineering.

**[An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management](https://press.stripe.com/an-elegant-puzzle)** by Will Larson
Practical advice on solving engineering management challenges: team sizing, migrations, organizational design, and more.

**[The Staff Engineer's Path](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-staff-engineers/9781098118723/)** by Tanya Reilly
Essential guide for staff+ engineers navigating the technical leadership track without direct reports.

**[Staff Engineer: Leadership beyond the management track](https://staffeng.com/book)** by Will Larson
Interviews and insights from staff engineers at major tech companies on how they reached and operate at that level.

**[High Output Management](https://www.amazon.com/High-Output-Management-Andrew-Grove/dp/0679762884)** by Andy Grove
Timeless classic on management from Intel's legendary CEO. Essential reading on leverage, meetings, and decision-making.

**[Radical Candor](https://www.radicalcandor.com/the-book/)** by Kim Scott
Framework for giving feedback and building trust with your team through caring personally and challenging directly.

**[Turn the Ship Around!](https://www.amazon.com/Turn-Ship-Around-Turning-Followers/dp/1591846404)** by L. David Marquet
Leadership lessons from a nuclear submarine captain on creating leaders at every level through intent-based leadership.

### System Design & Architecture

**[Designing Data-Intensive Applications](https://dataintensive.net/)** by Martin Kleppmann
The essential guide to distributed systems, databases, and building reliable, scalable applications. A must-read for staff+ engineers.

**[Building Microservices](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/building-microservices-2nd/9781492034018/)** by Sam Newman
Comprehensive guide to microservices architecture, from design to deployment and operations.

**[Fundamentals of Software Architecture](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/fundamentals-of-software/9781492043447/)** by Mark Richards & Neal Ford
Modern guide to software architecture patterns, trade-offs, and decision-making frameworks.

**[System Design Interview](https://www.amazon.com/System-Design-Interview-insiders-Second/dp/B08CMF2CQF)** by Alex Xu
Practical guide to system design with real-world examples. Useful for both interviewing and architectural thinking.

**[The Art of Scalability](https://theartofscalability.com/)** by Martin L. Abbott & Michael T. Fisher
Framework for scaling technology and organizations, covering technical, organizational, and process scalability.

### Software Engineering Excellence

**[A Philosophy of Software Design](https://web.stanford.edu/~ouster/cgi-bin/book.php)** by John Ousterhout
Focuses on complexity reduction and designing software that's easy to understand and modify.

**[The Pragmatic Programmer](https://pragprog.com/titles/tpp20/the-pragmatic-programmer-20th-anniversary-edition/)** by David Thomas & Andrew Hunt
Timeless advice on software craftsmanship, from code organization to career development.

**[Clean Architecture](https://www.amazon.com/Clean-Architecture-Craftsmans-Software-Structure/dp/0134494164)** by Robert C. Martin
Principles for creating maintainable, testable software systems that can evolve over time.

**[Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps](https://itrevolution.com/product/accelerate/)** by Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble & Gene Kim
Data-driven insights on what makes high-performing technology organizations based on four years of research.

**[The Phoenix Project](https://itrevolution.com/product/the-phoenix-project/)** by Gene Kim, Kevin Behr & George Spafford
Novel about DevOps transformation that illustrates principles of flow, feedback, and continuous learning.

### Organizational Culture & Strategy

**[The Five Dysfunctions of a Team](https://www.tablegroup.com/books/dysfunctions)** by Patrick Lencioni
Framework for understanding and addressing common team challenges: trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, and results.

**[Measure What Matters](https://www.whatmatters.com/the-book)** by John Doerr
Guide to OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) from the venture capitalist who introduced them to Google.

**[Good Strategy Bad Strategy](https://www.amazon.com/Good-Strategy-Bad-Difference-Matters/dp/0307886239)** by Richard Rumelt
Clarity on what makes strategy effective and how to develop good strategy for your organization.

**[Team Topologies](https://teamtopologies.com/book)** by Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais
Modern approach to organizing business and technology teams for fast flow and continuous delivery.

### Product & Innovation

**[The Lean Startup](https://theleanstartup.com/book)** by Eric Ries
Framework for building products through validated learning, experimentation, and iterative development.

**[Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love](https://www.svpg.com/books/inspired-how-to-create-tech-products-customers-love-2nd-edition/)** by Marty Cagan
Product management essentials from a Silicon Valley veteran on building products that matter.

**[The Innovator's Dilemma](https://www.amazon.com/Innovators-Dilemma-Technologies-Management-Innovation/dp/1633691780)** by Clayton Christensen
Classic on why great companies fail and how disruptive innovation reshapes industries.

## Articles & Essays

### Career Development

**[Being Glue](https://noidea.dog/glue)** by Tanya Reilly
Essential reading on the invisible work that holds teams together and why it's often undervalued.

**[StaffEng Stories](https://staffeng.com/stories)** - Multiple Authors
Collection of interviews with staff+ engineers at major tech companies about their career paths and daily work.

**[The Engineer/Manager Pendulum](https://charity.wtf/2017/05/11/the-engineer-manager-pendulum/)** by Charity Majors
Why it's valuable to swing between IC and management roles throughout your career.

**[Work on What Matters](https://staffeng.com/guides/work-on-what-matters)** by Will Larson
Framework for prioritizing work as a staff+ engineer: existential issues, editing, finishing, and what only you can do.

### Engineering Leadership

**[Leadership Comes From Everywhere](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/leadership-comes-from-everywhere)** by Gergely Orosz
How to demonstrate leadership regardless of your title or position.

**[Execution Is King](https://blog.bytebytego.com/p/execution-is-king)** - ByteByteGo
Why execution matters more than ideas and how to improve execution as a leader.

**[Write Five, Then Synthesize](https://www.facebook.com/notes/kent-beck/write-five-then-synthesize/1079924705386447/)** by Kent Beck
Approach to making technical decisions by exploring multiple options before converging.

**[Give Away Your Legos](https://review.firstround.com/give-away-your-legos-and-other-commandments-for-scaling-startups)** by Molly Graham
Why leaders need to let go of their most valuable work to scale themselves and their organizations.

**[On Being a Senior Engineer](https://www.kitchensoap.com/2012/10/25/on-being-a-senior-engineer/)** by John Allspaw

### Technical Decision Making

**[Architecture Decision Records](https://adr.github.io/)** by Michael Nygard
Why and how to document architectural decisions for future reference and learning.

**[Choose Boring Technology](https://boringtechnology.club/)** by Dan McKinley
Framework for making technology choices by limiting innovation tokens and choosing proven solutions.

**[Designing for Failure](https://www.infoq.com/articles/designing-failure/)** - Multiple Authors
Principles for building resilient distributed systems that gracefully handle failures.

**[The Twelve-Factor App](https://12factor.net/)** by Adam Wiggins
Methodology for building modern, scalable, maintainable SaaS applications.

### Platform Engineering & Distributed Systems

**[Distributed Systems for Fun and Profit](http://book.mixu.net/distsys/)** by Mikito Takada
Free online book covering distributed systems fundamentals in an accessible way.

**[Designing Uber's Tech Stack](https://eng.uber.com/tech-stack-part-one/)** - Uber Engineering
Multi-part series on building and scaling Uber's platform architecture.

**[Kafka: The Definitive Guide](https://www.confluent.io/resources/kafka-the-definitive-guide/)** by Neha Narkhede, Gwen Shapira & Todd Palino
Comprehensive guide to Apache Kafka for building real-time data pipelines.

**[Google SRE Books](https://sre.google/books/)** - Google SRE Team
Free books from Google on Site Reliability Engineering principles and practices.

**[Building Event-Driven Microservices](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/building-event-driven-microservices/9781492057888/)** by Adam Bellemare
Guide to event-driven architectures and building systems around event streams.

### Data & Analytics

**[The Data Warehouse Toolkit](https://www.kimballgroup.com/data-warehouse-business-intelligence-resources/books/data-warehouse-dw-toolkit/)** by Ralph Kimball & Margy Ross
Classic guide to dimensional modeling and data warehouse design.

**[Streaming Systems](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/streaming-systems/9781491983867/)** by Tyler Akidau, Slava Chernyak & Reuven Lax
Comprehensive guide to stream processing from Google engineers who built Dataflow.

**[The Semantic Layer](https://www.getdbt.com/blog/what-is-the-semantic-layer/)** by dbt Labs
Modern approach to building consistent business metrics and definitions.

### Team Management & Communication

**[Rands Leadership Slack](https://randsinrepose.com/welcome-to-rands-leadership-slack/)** - Rands in Repose
Active community of engineering leaders sharing experiences and advice.

**[1:1s Are My Most Important Meetings](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/1-1s-my-most-important-meetings-heres-how-i-run-them-steve-mundy/)** by Steve Mundy
Framework for running effective one-on-one meetings with your team.

**[How to Write Better with the Inverted Pyramid](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/inverted-pyramid/)** - Nielsen Norman Group
Writing technique for clear communication in technical documentation and updates.

**[Amazon's Writing Culture](https://www.inc.com/carmine-gallo/jeff-bezos-bans-powerpoint-in-meetings-his-replacement-is-brilliant.html)**
Why Amazon uses six-page narratives instead of PowerPoint and how it improves decision-making.

### Performance & Productivity

**[Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule](http://www.paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html)** by Paul Graham
Why managers and makers need different types of schedules and how to accommodate both.

**[Getting Things Done](https://gettingthingsdone.com/)** by David Allen
Productivity system for managing tasks, projects, and commitments effectively.

**[Deep Work](https://www.calnewport.com/books/deep-work/)** by Cal Newport
Why focused, uninterrupted work is becoming rare and valuable, and how to cultivate it.
